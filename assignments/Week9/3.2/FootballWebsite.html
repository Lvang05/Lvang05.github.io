Fun Facts About Football

The first American football game was played in 1869 between Princeton and Rutgers — and it looked more like soccer than today's game!

The NFL was founded in 1920 as the American Professional Football Association. It was renamed the National Football League in 1922.

Tom Brady has won more Super Bowl rings (7) than any single NFL franchise — including the Patriots and the Steelers!

The Dallas Cowboys are the most valuable NFL team, valued at over $9 billion — more than any soccer or basketball team in the world.

The Super Bowl is the most-watched TV event in the U.S. every year — with over 100 million viewers tuning in annually.

The shortest overtime in NFL history lasted just 11 seconds — in 2014 when the Broncos scored a touchdown immediately after kickoff.

History

American football originated in the late 19th century, evolving from two English sports — rugby and soccer. The first recorded game was played in 1869 between Rutgers and Princeton, but the sport truly began to take shape in the 1880s thanks to rule changes introduced by Walter Camp, often called the "Father of American Football." Camp established the line of scrimmage, the down-and-distance system, and other fundamental rules that gave the game a more structured and strategic identity. By the early 1900s, college football had exploded in popularity, though it also faced criticism for violence and injuries, which led to reforms and the eventual creation of the NCAA in 1906.

Professional football began gaining traction in the early 20th century, and in 1920, the American Professional Football Association was formed — later renamed the National Football League (NFL) in 1922. For decades, the NFL competed with other leagues, most notably the AFL, which eventually merged with the NFL in 1970. This merger gave rise to the modern Super Bowl and a unified, nationwide league. Since then, the NFL has grown into a multi-billion dollar industry, with the Super Bowl becoming a major cultural event. American football today is a blend of athleticism, strategy, and entertainment — and remains one of the most-watched and passionately followed sports in the United States.

Rules

Understanding the rules of American football is essential for both players and fans. Here are some of the key rules to know:

  • Four Downs: Each team has four attempts (downs) to advance the ball 10 yards. If successful, they earn a new set of downs.
  • Scoring: A touchdown is worth 6 points, followed by an extra point (1) or a two-point conversion. Field goals are worth 3 points.
  • Kickoff: Games start with a kickoff, and after each score, the ball is kicked to the opposing team to begin their possession.
  • Offsides & False Starts: Players cannot cross the line of scrimmage before the ball is snapped. Doing so results in a penalty.
  • Pass Interference: Defenders cannot make contact with a receiver before the ball arrives. This results in a significant yardage penalty.
  • Turnovers: Fumbles and interceptions result in a change of possession, giving the opposing team control of the ball.
  • Game Length: A standard NFL game consists of four 15-minute quarters, with a halftime break between the second and third quarters.
